Recognition: It is a very male dominated sport, only as of the recent times has the women's division flourished and bloomed. This being said, the women's Brazilian Jiu-jitsu division has shown the world the kind of community and support it offers. Empowered women empower women truly comes to life within the confines of the BJJ women's league.


Equipment

Gi: The gi is the equivalent of a kimono. However, the gi is different due to the thickness of the cotton making the texture very rough to the touch. This is why you will see BJJ Gi athletes with calluses on the knuckles and swollen joints.

Pros

  • Traditional
  • Cerebral
  • Large quantity of academies

    • Cons

  • initially costly because of equipment
  • Arthritis
  • Dying form of Martial Art

No-Gi: The BJJ practice that requires no-gi; it is the practice with skin tight article of clothing such as rashgaurds,shorts, and spats. The technique is vastly different from the gi.

Pros

  • Popularized in MMA
  • Affordable
  • More income opportunities for competitors

    • Cons

  • Branched away from traditional gi
  • No belt system
  • Smaller pool of competitions available

Belt Ranks

Seasoned Champions - Gi

Marcus "Buchecha" Almeida is one of the best perfoming Brazilian Jiu-jitsu athletes of all time. his W-L-D ratio is 137-13-1.He has both competed in Gi and no Gi

Leandro "Lenda" Lo was one of the best Brazilian Jiu-jitsu athletes of all time. He was a champion in four different weight categories within a single year. His W-L-D is 271-43-6. Unfortunately he passed away the 8th of August 2022, due to gunshot wound. May he rest in peace.

Rubens 'cobrinha' Charles is a multi-champion competitor in both no-gi and gi. His W-L-D ratio is 120-21-0. He is known for being very talented in the guard position.

Gabi Garcia is one of the most success female competitors of all time, keeping a win streak of W-L-D ratio: 25-1-0. She is also very proud of and supportive of the women's bjj division. She is always encouraging those to feel comfortable, strong and confident through learning a beautiful art like BJJ.

A silent but deadly assassin of a competitor. Mayssa Bastos is one of the most tenatious, Brazilian Jiu-jitsu athletes of this generation. She is a multiple world champion in both gi and no gi, in the rooster and feather weight division (lightest divisions). Her W-L-D ratio is: 89-15-0.

Gabi Pessanha is becoming the greatest of the newcoming generation. At only the age of 22, she has become a 4x black belt world champion, 4x heavy weight Abu Dhabi champion and much more. Her W-L-D is: 51-2-0
